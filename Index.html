About Pushkaralu

Pushkaralu is one of the most sacred river festivals in India, celebrated once every 12 years for each major river. It holds immense spiritual significance and attracts millions of devotees from across the country.

The festival begins when the planet Jupiter (Guru) enters a specific zodiac sign associated with a river. During this period, taking a holy dip in the river is believed to purify the soul and remove past sins.

The Godavari Pushkaralu is especially important, as the Godavari River is known as the “Dakshina Ganga” (Ganges of the South). Devotees gather at various ghats to perform rituals, prayers, and offerings to their ancestors.

  • 🛕 Sacred river bathing (Pushkara Snanam)
  • 🙏 Special poojas and religious rituals
  • 🕯️ Offering prayers for ancestors (Pinda Pradanam)
  • 🎭 Cultural and spiritual programs
  • 🍛 Free food services (Annadanam)
